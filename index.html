What is DermaScope AI

Meet DermaScopeAI – your intelligent skin health companion. DermaScopeAI harnesses the power of deep learning and computer vision to detect and assess the severity of common skin conditions like acne, eczema, and psoriasis. From identifying lesions to grading disease severity, DermaScopeAI delivers fast, reliable, and objective results. Built with advanced AI models tailored for each skin condition, the system ensures high accuracy across diverse skin tones and lighting conditions. Whether for clinical use or self-monitoring, DermaScopeAI empowers better skin care decisions with precision and ease.

What do we provide ?

Skin Disease Detection

Instantly detect acne, eczema, and psoriasis from uploaded skin images using powerful AI-driven analysis.

Severity Grading

Get accurate severity scores for your diagnosed skin condition to guide treatment decisions effectively.

Multi-Condition Monitoring

Track changes over time and monitor multiple conditions within a single platform for holistic skin health management.

Our domain

Literature Survey

Skin diseases are among the most prevalent health issues globally, with conditions like acne, eczema, and psoriasis affecting millions of people. Traditional diagnostic methods heavily rely on visual inspection by dermatologists, leading to variability and subjectivity in diagnosis and grading of severity. Recent advancements in AI and machine learning have introduced powerful computer-aided diagnostic (CAD) systems capable of analyzing clinical images with high accuracy and consistency. DermaScopeAI builds on these developments by integrating deep learning models for image analysis, lesion detection, and severity classification, aiming for a more objective and scalable solution.

Research Gap

Despite significant progress, existing automated skin disease detection systems face limitations in generalizability, explainability, and severity grading. Many solutions lack diversity in training datasets, impacting performance across various skin tones and lesion types. Additionally, most systems focus either on detection or grading—not both. DermaScopeAI addresses these gaps by using diverse datasets, enhancing model interpretability through explainable AI, and integrating both detection and severity classification in a single robust framework.

Research Problem

Chronic skin diseases such as eczema, psoriasis, and acne cause significant physical and psychological distress. Accurate and timely diagnosis, along with reliable severity grading, is critical for effective treatment. Traditional diagnostic methods are subjective, prone to inter-clinician variability, and time-consuming. Automated systems exist but are limited by dataset diversity, real-time processing, and lack of interpretability. The main research problem is to develop a scalable, accurate, and explainable AI-based solution for both detection and severity classification of these skin conditions.

Research Objectives

  1. Develop an AI-driven diagnostic system for acne, eczema, and psoriasis detection.
  2. Integrate severity assessment models for accurate grading of detected conditions.
  3. Enhance model interpretability using Explainable AI (XAI) techniques for clinical trust.
  4. Achieve high generalizability across different skin tones, lesion types, and lighting conditions.
  5. Facilitate real-time, scalable dermatological care accessible in clinical and remote settings.

Methodology

  1. Acne Detection and Severity: VGG16-based model optimized for computational efficiency, combined with a custom CNN for multi-class severity classification.
  2. Eczema Detection and Severity: A custom CNN with Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) blocks for better feature refinement, paired with mobilenetv2 for severity classification.
  3. Psoriasis Detection and Severity: YOLOv8 for real-time lesion detection, alongside MobileNetV2 with MixConv for precise severity assessment.
Preprocessing techniques such as image normalization, augmentation, and class balancing are applied to enhance model performance. Explainability is achieved through Grad-CAM, allowing clinicians to visualize critical regions influencing model decisions.
